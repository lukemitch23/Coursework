<h2>Actual sensor size</h2>
<div class="set_size relative font_smaller">
<span class="ac_note">Note:</span> Actual size is set to <span class="diag bold"></span> screen → <a class="change_btn" href="/specs/nikon_z9/#change_screen_size">change »</a>
</div>
<div class="actual_size font_smaller">
<div class="sensor_size" data-sheight="23.90" data-swidth="35.90" style="height:23.90mm;width:35.90mm;">
</div>
<div class="center pad">This is the actual size of the <b>Z9</b> sensor: <u style="white-space:nowrap;">35.9 x 23.9 mm</u></div>
<div class="sen_info">
        The sensor has a surface area of <b>858</b> mm².
        There are approx. 45,700,000 photosites (pixels) on this area.
        Pixel pitch, which is a measure of the distance between pixels, is <b>4.34</b> µm.
        Pixel pitch tells you the distance from the center of one pixel (photosite) to the center of the next.
        <br/><br/>

        Pixel or photosite area is <b>18.84</b> µm².
        The larger the photosite, the more light it can capture and the more information can be recorded.

        <br/><br/>
        Pixel density tells you how many million pixels fit or would fit in one square cm of the sensor.
        Nikon Z9 has a pixel density of
        <b>5.32</b> MP/cm².
        <br/><br/>
        These numbers are important in terms of assessing the overall quality of a digital camera.
        Generally, the bigger (and newer) the sensor, pixel pitch and photosite area, and the smaller the pixel density,
        the better the camera.

<div id="diagonal">
<h2>Diagonal</h2>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
    
 Diagonal is calculated by the use of Pythagorean theorem:
  
  <table style="margin-top:0.5em;margin-bottom:0.5em;">
<tr>
<td class="nowrap_td right_txt">
<i>Diagonal</i> = <span style="font-size: 121%;">√</span>
</td><td class="nowrap_td" style="border-top:solid 1px black;">
             <i>w</i>² + <i>h</i>²
        </td>
</tr>
</table>
 
 where <i>w</i> = sensor width and  <i>h</i> = sensor height
 <br/><br/><br/>
</div>
<h3 class="bold">Nikon Z9 diagonal:</h3>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
<i>w</i> = 35.90 mm<br/>
<i>h</i> = 23.90 mm
 <br/>
<table style="margin-top:0.5em;margin-bottom:0.5em;">
<tr>
<td class="nowrap_td right_txt">
<i>Diagonal</i> = <span style="font-size: 121%;">√</span>
</td>
<td class="nowrap_td" style="border-top:solid 1px black;">
             35.90² + 23.90²
        </td>
<td>
           = <b><u>43.13 mm</u></b>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>
<br/><br/>
</div>
<div id="area">
<h2>Surface area</h2>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
  Surface area is calculated by multiplying the width and the height of a sensor. 
  <br/><br/>
<i>Width</i> = 35.90 mm<br/>
<i>Height</i> = 23.90 mm<br/><br/>
<i>Surface area</i> = 
  35.90 × 23.90 = <b><u>858.01 mm²</u></b>
</div>
<br/><br/>
</div>
<div id="pitch">
<h2>Pixel pitch</h2>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
Pixel pitch is the distance from the center of one pixel to the center of the 
next measured in micrometers (µm). It can be calculated with the following formula:

<table class="fraction">
<tr>
<td class="nowrap_td" rowspan="2">
<i>Pixel pitch</i> =    
        </td>
<td class="nowrap_td">
<i>sensor width in mm</i>
</td>
<td class="nowrap_td" rowspan="2">
             × <i>1000</i>
</td>
</tr><tr>
<td class="upper_line">
<i>sensor resolution width in pixels</i>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>
<br/>
<h3 class="bold">Nikon Z9 pixel pitch:</h3>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
<i>Sensor width</i> = 35.90 mm<br/>
<i>Sensor resolution width</i> = 8280 pixels<br/>
<table class="fraction">
<tr>
<td class="nowrap_td" rowspan="2">
<i>Pixel pitch</i> =    
        </td>
<td class="nowrap_td">
<i>35.90</i>
</td>
<td class="nowrap_td" rowspan="2">
             × <i>1000</i>
</td>
<td class="nowrap_td" rowspan="2">
             = <b><u>4.34 µm</u></b>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td class="upper_line">
<i>8280</i>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>
<br/><br/>
</div>
<div id="photosite">
<h2>Pixel area</h2>
<div class="pad font_smaller">
The area of one pixel can be calculated by simply squaring the pixel pitch:
<br/>
<div class="center" style="margin-top:10px;"><i>Pixel area</i> = <i>pixel pitch</i>²</div>
<br/>
You could also divide sensor surface area with effective megapixels:
<br/>
<table class="fraction">
